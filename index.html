How it works

A friendly martial-arts movement game for a 7-year-old, built around Taekwondo and animal styles. Most of the work is animal flow: five Animal Styles — Bear, Crab, Monkey, Flamingo and Cat — each a small family of real bodyweight moves, trained in the dojang. The first two moves of every style are open from day one (so there's always variety); moves 3–5 unlock as he masters the one before. Mastering a move takes a set number of separate days of practice — tapping "I practiced this" counts once per day, which rewards showing up regularly and keeps each day light and easy. Mastered moves chain together into Combos. There's also a hidden Striker's Path of simple Taekwondo basics.

  • Every day (~5 min): the Dojang Warm-Up — gentle wrist and ankle prep, a few slow breaths, and the week's featured move. Builds the streak and earns a star.
  • Two short sessions a week (Mon & Fri, ~15 min): a few different animal styles plus a combo. Quality over quantity.
  • Wednesday: his Taekwondo class day — the app just suggests a quick warm-up beforehand.
  • Sunday: a full rest day.

Good form & safety

Every cue favors steady, controlled movement. For the animal moves you'll see "soft elbows — don't lock," "stack your joints," and "don't sink." For striking you'll see "breathe out on the strike," "re-chamber every kick," and "control it — don't throw it."

All strikes are practiced in the air or on a pad — never on a person. Kicks stay low to medium, only as high as he can control, with a soft landing every time. Give him lots of open space, and lean on the Taekwondo values of focus, courtesy and perseverance. If a move ever feels wrong, skip it and pick another style — variety is built in on purpose.

Save & back up progress

Progress is stored on this device. Use Export to save a backup file, or to move it to another device. The Google Sheet sync is the next add-on (see below).

Optional — Google Sheet tracking: this build can send each completed session to a Google Sheet via an Apps Script web-app URL (no IDE needed). The hook is already wired in the code (SHEET_WEBAPP_URL near the top of the script) — paste your deployed URL there to turn it on.
